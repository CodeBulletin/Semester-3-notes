Terminologies

Population: The entire group or set of individuals, objects, or events that a researcher wants to study or draw conclusions about.

Sample: A subset of the population that is selected for data collection and analysis. The characteristics of the sample are used to make inferences about the population.

Variable: A characteristic or attribute that can take different values. Variables can be classified as independent (predictor) or dependent (response) variables.

Data: The values or observations collected for one or more variables. Data can be numerical (quantitative) or categorical (qualitative).

Descriptive Statistics: Techniques used to summarize and describe the main features of a dataset. Examples include measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and measures of variability (range, standard deviation).

Inferential Statistics: Methods used to make inferences and predictions about a population based on sample data. Examples include hypothesis testing and confidence intervals.

Hypothesis: A testable statement or claim about a population parameter that researchers aim to support or reject through data analysis.

Hypothesis Testing: A statistical procedure used to determine whether there is enough evidence to support or reject a hypothesis based on sample data.

Confidence Interval: A range of values constructed around a sample statistic, such as a mean or proportion, which is likely to contain the population parameter with a certain level of confidence.

Sampling: The process of selecting a subset (sample) from the larger group (population) for data collection and analysis.

Probability: The likelihood of an event or outcome occurring. It is used to study random processes and uncertainty.

Correlation: A statistical measure that indicates the degree of association between two variables. It can be positive, negative, or zero.

Regression: A statistical method used to model the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.

Outlier: An extreme value that significantly differs from other values in a dataset and may distort the overall analysis.

Data Distribution: The pattern of how data values are spread across a range of possible values. Common distributions include normal, uniform, and exponential distributions.

P-value: The probability of obtaining results as extreme or more extreme than those observed if the null hypothesis is true. It is used in hypothesis testing to determine statistical significance.
