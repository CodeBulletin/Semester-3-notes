Types of Statistics

Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics summarize data informatively, providing an overview without inferring beyond the dataset. Key techniques include:

  • Measures of Central Tendency: Mean (average), median (middle value), and mode (most frequent value).
  • Measures of Variability: Range (max-min difference), standard deviation (data spread from mean), and variance (standard deviation squared).
  • Frequency Distributions: Tables or graphs showing value occurrences.
  • Graphical Representations: Histograms (frequency of continuous data), bar charts (categorical data), and pie charts (proportions in a dataset).

Descriptive statistics help understand a dataset's shape, distribution, central values, and variability, foundational for advanced techniques like inferential statistics.

Inferential Statistics

IInferential statistics make population predictions from sample data, involving:

  • Formulating Hypotheses: Creating testable statements about the population.
  • Selecting a Sample: Choosing a representative sample with unbiased sampling techniques.
  • Collecting Data: Gathering data through surveys, experiments, or observations.
  • Analyzing Data: Applying methods like hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis to estimate population parameters.
  • Drawing Conclusions: Making population conclusions based on sample data analysis.
  • Generalizing to Population: Using sample findings to infer about the broader population, acknowledging uncertainty.

Inferential statistics is crucial in various fields for data-driven decisions and insights into larger populations, requiring careful interpretation to ensure conclusion validity.
