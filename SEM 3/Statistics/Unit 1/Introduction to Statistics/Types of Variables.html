Types of Variables

Categorical Variables:

  • Nominal: Represent categories with no order (e.g., colors, gender, types of animals).
  • Ordinal: Have an order but uneven intervals (e.g., education levels, satisfaction ratings, socioeconomic classes).

Quantitative Variables:

  • Discrete: Distinct values, typically whole numbers (e.g., number of cars, students in a class, customer complaints).
  • Continuous: Infinite values within a range, including decimals (e.g., height, weight, temperature, time).
