Types of data

Data analytics involves analyzing various data types, each with unique characteristics:

  1. Structured Data: Highly organized, follows a predefined format, easily processed by machines. Includes numerical, categorical, and time-series data, commonly stored in databases or spreadsheets.
  2. Unstructured Data: Lacks a predefined format, includes text, social media posts, emails, images, videos, and audio. Analysis often requires natural language processing and computer vision.
  3. Semi-Structured Data: Has some organizational properties but lacks a rigid structure. Examples include XML files, JSON data, and log files.
  4. Big Data: Extremely large and complex datasets, challenging for traditional processing methods. Involves high volume, velocity, and variety, analyzed using frameworks like Apache Hadoop and Apache Spark.
  5. Time-Series Data: Data collected over time at regular intervals, used for forecasting and trend analysis. Examples are stock prices and weather data.
  6. Geospatial Data: Associated with geographic locations, includes coordinates, maps, and satellite imagery, used in urban planning and logistics.
  7. Streaming Data: Continuously generated and processed in real-time, from sources like sensors, IoT devices, and social media.

The choice of data type depends on the specific analytical problem and available data sources.
