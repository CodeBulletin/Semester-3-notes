Median

In statistics, the "median" is another measure of central tendency that represents the middle value of a dataset when it's arranged in ascending or descending order. Unlike the mean, which is affected by extreme values (outliers), the median is resistant to outliers and gives a better sense of the typical value in a distribution.

To calculate the median:

  1. Organize the Data: Arrange your data points in ascending or descending order.
  2. Identify the Middle Value(s): If the number of data points  \(n\) is odd, the median is the middle value directly. If  \(n\) is even, the median is the average of the two middle values.

Let's go through an example:

  1. Suppose you have the following data set: 8, 12, 15, 19, 22
  2. Organize the data in ascending order: 8, 12, 15, 19, 22
  3. Since the total number of data points (\(n\)) is odd (5), the median is the middle value, which is the third value in this case: 15

Now let's consider an example with an even number of data points:

  1. Data set: 6, 10, 14, 18, 20, 26
  2. Organize the data in ascending order: 6, 10, 14, 18, 20, 26
  3. Since the total number of data points (\(n\)) is even (6), the median is the average of the two middle values, which are the third and fourth values: \(\frac{14 + 18}2 = \frac{32}2 = 16\)

In both cases, the median provides a measure of central tendency that is not influenced by extreme values, making it a good choice when the data might contain outliers.

Keep in mind that when dealing with grouped data or continuous distributions, the median can be calculated in a similar manner but with additional considerations.
