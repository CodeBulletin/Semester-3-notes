Mean Deviation

Mean deviation is a measure of dispersion that quantifies the average distance between each data point in a dataset and the mean or median of that dataset. It provides information about the spread or variability of the data.

Mean Deviation with Mean

Mean deviation with respect to the mean is a measure of dispersion that quantifies the average distance between each data point in a dataset and the mean of that dataset. It provides information about the spread or variability of the data around the mean.

To calculate the mean deviation with respect to the mean, you follow these steps:

  1. Calculate the mean of the dataset.
  2. Find the absolute difference between each data point and the mean.
  3. Sum up all the absolute differences.
  4. Divide the sum by the total number of data points.

Formula: \(MD_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sum^C_{i=1}f_i|x_i-\bar{x}|}{\sum^C_{i=1}f_i}\)

For example, let's consider the dataset: 5, 8, 10, 12, 16.

  1. Calculate the mean: (5 + 8 + 10 + 12 + 16) / 5 = 10.2
  2. Find the absolute difference between each data point and the mean: |5 - 10.2| = 4.8, |8 - 10.2| = 1.8, |10 - 10.2| = 0.2, |12 - 10.2| = 1.8, |16 - 10.2| = 5.8
  3. Sum up all the absolute differences: 4.8 + 1.8 + 0.2 + 1.8 + 5.8 = 14.4
  4. Divide the sum by the total number of data points: 14.4 / 5 = 2.88

Therefore, the mean deviation with respect to the mean for this dataset is 2.88.

Mean Deviation with Median (Use this by default)

Mean deviation with respect to the median is a measure of dispersion that quantifies the average distance between each data point in a dataset and the median of that dataset. It provides information about the spread or variability of the data around the median

To calculate the mean deviation with respect to the median, you follow similar steps as above, but instead of using the mean, you use the median.

Formula: \(MD_{M} = \frac{\sum^C_{i=1}f_i|x_i-M|}{\sum^C_{i=1}f_i}\)

For example, let's consider the dataset: 5, 8, 10, 12, 16.

  1. Arrange the dataset in ascending order: 5, 8, 10, 12, 16.
  2. Find the median: The median is 10.
  3. Find the absolute difference between each data point and the median: |5 - 10| = 5 |8 - 10| = 2 |10 - 10| = 0 |12 - 10| = 2 |16 - 10| = 6
  4. Sum up all the absolute differences: 5 + 2 + 0 + 2 + 6 = 15.
  5. Divide the sum by the total number of data points: 15 / 5 = 3.

Therefore, the mean deviation with respect to the median for this dataset is 3.

In both cases, the mean deviation provides a measure of the average distance between each data point and the central tendency (mean or median) of the dataset. It helps to understand the dispersion or variability of the data points.

The coefficient of mean deviation with respect to the mean and median

The coefficient of mean deviation with respect to the mean and median is a measure that allows for the comparison of the mean deviations of different datasets, regardless of their scales or units. It provides a standardized measure of dispersion.

Coefficient of Mean Deviation with Mean

To calculate the coefficient of mean deviation with respect to the mean, you divide the mean deviation with the mean of the dataset.

Coefficient of Mean Deviation with Mean = (Mean Deviation with Mean / Mean)

Coefficient of Mean Deviation with Median

To calculate the coefficient of mean deviation with respect to the median, you divide the mean deviation with the median of the dataset.

Coefficient of Mean Deviation with Median = (Mean Deviation with Median / Median)

The coefficient of mean deviation provides a relative measure of dispersion that allows for the comparison of datasets with different scales or units. A smaller coefficient indicates a lower relative dispersion, while a larger coefficient suggests a higher relative dispersion. It helps in comparing the variability of datasets and understanding the relative spread of data points around the mean or median.
