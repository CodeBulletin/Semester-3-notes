Marascuilo procedure

The Marascuilo procedure is a statistical method used for making pairwise comparisons of proportions when dealing with more than two groups. It is particularly useful when you have conducted a Chi-Square Test of Homogeneity and found significant differences, but you need to know exactly which pairs of proportions are significantly different. This procedure allows you to simultaneously compare multiple pairs of proportions to identify where the significant differences lie.

Key Elements of the Marascuilo Procedure:

Purpose: To identify which specific pairs of proportions are significantly different after a Chi-Square Test of Homogeneity indicates that there are differences among three or more groups.

Procedure:

  • Conduct a Chi-Square Test of Homogeneity and determine if there are significant differences among the proportions.
  • If the test is significant, calculate the critical values for each pairwise comparison using the Marascuilo formula.
  • Compare the absolute difference between each pair of sample proportions to the corresponding critical value. If the difference is greater than the critical value, the difference between those two proportions is significant.

Interpretation: This procedure identifies specific pairs of proportions that are significantly different, helping to pinpoint where the differences lie among multiple groups.

Assumptions:

  • The samples from each population are independent.
  • The data are categorical.
  • The sample size is sufficiently large for the Chi-Square distribution to be an appropriate approximation.

Applications:

  • Marketing Research: To compare proportions of customer preferences across different demographic groups.
  • Medical Studies: To compare treatment success rates across different treatment groups.
  • Sociological Research: To examine differences in proportions of responses to survey questions across different social groups.

Limitations:

  • The procedure requires a large sample size for accurate results.
  • It only applies to categorical data.
  • If many comparisons are made, the risk of Type I error (false positive) increases, although the Marascuilo procedure does control for this to some extent.
