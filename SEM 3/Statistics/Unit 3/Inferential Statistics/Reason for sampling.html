Reason for sampling

Sampling is a technique used in statistics to select a subset of individuals or items from a larger population. There are several reasons for using sampling:

  1. Practicality: In many cases, it is not feasible or practical to collect data from an entire population due to factors such as time, cost, or accessibility. Sampling allows researchers to gather information from a smaller, more manageable subset of the population.
  2. Cost-effectiveness: Conducting a study or survey on an entire population can be expensive. By using sampling, researchers can reduce costs by collecting data from a representative sample instead of the entire population.
  3. Time efficiency: Collecting data from an entire population can be time-consuming. Sampling allows researchers to obtain results more quickly by focusing on a smaller subset of the population.
  4. Accuracy: When done correctly, sampling can provide accurate and reliable estimates of population characteristics. By using appropriate sampling techniques, researchers can ensure that the selected sample is representative of the population, allowing for valid inferences to be made.
  5. Feasibility of data collection: In some cases, it may be difficult or impossible to collect data from the entire population. For example, when studying rare diseases or inaccessible populations, sampling provides a practical way to gather information.
