Inferential Statistics

Inferential statistics is a branch of statistics that involves drawing conclusions or making inferences about a population based on a sample of data. It is used to make predictions, test hypotheses, and generalize findings from a smaller group (sample) to a larger population. Inferential statistics relies on probability theory and statistical techniques to analyze and interpret data, allowing researchers to make informed decisions and draw meaningful conclusions about the population of interest. Some common inferential statistical methods include hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis.
