Normal Distribution


The normal distribution, also known as the Gaussian distribution or bell curve, is one of the most fundamental and widely used probability distributions in statistics. It describes the distribution of a continuous random variable that has certain characteristics:

  1. Symmetry: The normal distribution is symmetric, which means it is shaped like a bell curve. The peak of the curve is at the mean (average) of the distribution, and the curve is symmetrical around this point.
  2. Mean and Median: The mean, median, and mode of a normal distribution are all equal and located at the center of the distribution.
  3. Bell-Shaped: The probability density function of the normal distribution has a single, unimodal peak, and it tapers off gradually as you move away from the mean. The tails of the distribution extend infinitely in both directions.
  4. Parameters: The normal distribution is characterized by two parameters:
    • Mean (μ): This parameter determines the center or location of the distribution.
    • Standard Deviation (σ): This parameter controls the spread or width of the distribution. Larger values of σ result in wider distributions, while smaller values result in narrower distributions.

\(P(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}*e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}\)

\(P(a \le x \le b) = \int_{a}^{b}{(\frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}*e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}})dx}\)

The normal distribution is important in statistics for several reasons:

  1. Many natural phenomena and measurements, such as the heights of individuals, test scores, and errors in measurements, tend to follow a normal distribution.
  2. The central limit theorem states that the sum (or average) of a large number of independent, identically distributed random variables will be approximately normally distributed, regardless of the original distribution of those variables. This makes the normal distribution applicable in various statistical inference and hypothesis testing scenarios.
  3. It provides a foundation for various statistical techniques, such as hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis.
  4. Many statistical and machine learning models assume that the residuals (errors) of the model follow a normal distribution.
  5. The normal distribution is a fundamental concept in statistics and plays a crucial role in understanding and analyzing data in various fields of science, engineering, social sciences, and finance.
