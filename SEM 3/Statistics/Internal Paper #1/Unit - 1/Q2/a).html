(a) The Hawaii Visitors Bureau collects data on visitors to Hawaii. The following questions were among 16 asked in a questionnaire handed out to passengers during incoming airline flights.

  • This trip to Hawaii is my: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, etc.
  • The primary reason for this trip is: (10 categories, including vacation, convention, honeymoon). 
  • Where I plan to stay: (11 categories, including hotel, apartment, relatives, camping).
  • Total days in Hawaii.
  1. What is the population being studied?
  2. Is the use of a questionnaire a good way to reach the population of passengers on incoming airline flights?
  3. Comment on each of the four questions in terms of whether it will provide categorical or quantitative data.

(I)

The population being studied in this case is the group of passengers on incoming airline flights to Hawaii. This group includes tourists, business travelers, and any other individuals arriving in Hawaii via airline flights.

(II)

Using a questionnaire handed out during incoming airline flights is a generally effective method for reaching this specific population. Since all passengers on these flights are part of the target population, distributing questionnaires during flights ensures direct access to a relevant and captive audience. Additionally, the context of being on a flight to Hawaii might make respondents more inclined to provide accurate and thoughtful answers related to their trip.

(III)

This trip to Hawaii is my: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, etc.

  • This question provides quantitative data. It asks for a numeric value indicating the number of times the respondent has visited Hawaii.

The primary reason for this trip is: (10 categories, including vacation, convention, honeymoon).

  • This question yields categorical data. It asks the respondent to select their primary reason for visiting from a set list of categories.

Where I plan to stay: (11 categories, including hotel, apartment, relatives, camping).

  • This question also provides categorical data. It requires respondents to choose their accommodation type from predefined categories.

Total days in Hawaii.

  • This question produces quantitative data as it asks for a specific number, representing the total days the respondent plans to spend in Hawaii.
