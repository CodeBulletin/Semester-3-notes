Job Sequencing with Deadlines

The Job Sequencing with Deadlines problem is another classic optimization problem that involves scheduling a set of jobs with associated profits and deadlines to maximize the total profit. Each job has a specific deadline by which it needs to be completed, and only one job can be scheduled at a time also one job requires 1 unit of time.

Here's a step-by-step approach to solving the Job Sequencing with Deadlines problem:

  1. Sort the jobs in descending order based on their profits.
  2. Initialize an array or list to keep track of the time slots. Each time slot represents a specific deadline.
  3. Iterate through the sorted jobs and do the following:
    • Starting from the job's deadline, find the first available time slot (i.e., a time slot that is not yet occupied).
    • If a time slot is available, assign the job to that time slot and mark it as occupied.
    • If no time slot is available, skip the job (as it cannot be scheduled within its deadline).
  4. Calculate the total profit by summing up the profits of the scheduled jobs.

 

\(Time \space Complexity = O(n \space log \space n + n * d)\),

Where,

\(n\) is number of jobs

\(d\) is maximum deadline
