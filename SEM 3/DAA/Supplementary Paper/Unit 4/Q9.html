(a) Define terms: P-Class, NP-Class, NP-Hard and NP-Complete.

  1. P (Polynomial Time): P is the class of decision problems that can be solved by a deterministic Turing machine in polynomial time. These are problems that have efficient algorithms for their solution.
  2. NP (Nondeterministic Polynomial Time): NP is the class of decision problems that can be verified in polynomial time. While finding a solution to an NP problem may be difficult, given a potential solution, it can be efficiently verified. The question of whether P = NP, i.e., whether every problem in NP can be solved in polynomial time, is one of the most important open problems in computer science.
  3. NP-complete: NP-complete is a subset of NP problems that are considered to be among the most difficult problems in NP. A problem is NP-complete if every other problem in NP can be reduced to it in polynomial time. If a polynomial-time algorithm is found for any NP-complete problem, it would imply that P = NP.
  4. NP-hard: NP-hard is a class of problems that are at least as difficult as the NP-complete problems. While NP-complete problems are a subset of NP-hard problems, NP-hard problems may or may not be in NP. They represent problems that are computationally challenging and do not necessarily have efficient solutions.
