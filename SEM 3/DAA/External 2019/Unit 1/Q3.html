Q3

(a) The general form of the master method is given as \(T(n) = aT(n/b) + f(n)\). Explain the meaning of the terms \(n\), \(a\) and \(f(n)\). Solve the equation for the case when \(a = b\) and \(f(n) = kn\).

The Master Theorem is a tool in computer science used to determine the asymptotic complexity of recurrence relations of the form:

\(T(n)=aT(n/b)+f(n)\)

where:

  • \(a \ge 1\) is the number of subproblems in the recursion.
  • \(n/b\) is the size of each subproblem. (Here, \(b > 1\))
  • \(f(n)\) is the cost of the work done outside the recursive calls.

The Master Theorem helps in finding the time complexity of divide and conquer algorithms, but it's not always applicable.
