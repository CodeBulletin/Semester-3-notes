Q2

(a) Give the conditions where master method is not applicable supported by giving suitable examples.

Conditions Where Master Theorem Does Not Apply

If \(a<1\) or  \(b \le 1\): The theorem assumes that the number of subproblems and the reduction factor in the problem size are significant. If  \(a < 1\), it implies there are less than one subproblem per recursive call, which is non-sensical. If \(b≤1\), it means the problem size doesn't decrease, leading to infinite recursion.

Non-Polynomial Difference between \(f(n)\) and  \(n ^ {log_{⁡b}(a)}\): If the function \(f(n)\) is not polynomially smaller or larger than \(n ^ {log_{⁡b}(a)}\), the Master Theorem doesn't apply. For example, if \(f(n)\) is in the form of exponential functions, factorial functions, or others that don't have a polynomial relationship with \(n ^ {log_{⁡b}(a)}\).

Irregular Recurrence Relations: If the recurrence relation has a non-standard form, i.e., it does not fit the form \(aT(n/b)+f(n)\), the Master Theorem cannot be applied. For example

Non-Constant Parameters \(a\) and  \(b\): If \(a\) and  \(b\) are not constants, but functions of \(n\), the Master Theorem is not applicable.

Examples

Example Where \(a < 1\):

  • \(T(n)=0.5T(n/2)+n\)
  • Here, since \(a < 1\), the Master Theorem does not apply.

Example with Non-Polynomial Difference:

  • \(T(n)=2T(n/2)+2^n\).
  • Here, \(f(n) = 2^n\) is not polynomially smaller or larger than \(n ^ {log_{⁡b}(a)} = n\).

Non-Constant Parameters Example:

  • \(T(n)=nT(\frac n {\sqrt n})+n\)
  • Here, \(a = n\) and  \(b = \sqrt n\) are not constants.
