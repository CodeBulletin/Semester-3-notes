Solution of Transcendental and Polynomial Equations

Transcendental Equation: A transcendental equation involves transcendental functions, which are not expressible as finite combinations of basic algebraic operations and integer exponentiation. These include trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions. Common in various scientific fields, they are often more complex to solve than polynomial equations and may require numerical or iterative methods. Examples are equations like \(\text{sin}(x) = 0.5\),  \(e^x=1\) or \(\text{log(x)} = 1\)

Polynomial Equation: A polynomial equation involves a polynomial expression, which is made up of variables, coefficients, and non-negative integer exponents. These equations, written as  \(P(x) = 0\), seek values of \(x\) that satisfy the equation. Polynomial expressions are sums of terms with varying powers of \(x\). The degree of the polynomial influences its properties and solutions. Polynomial equations are central in mathematics and have widespread applications.

Iterative Methods: Iterative methods are techniques used to solve complex mathematical and scientific problems by refining initial approximations in a series of steps. They are essential in areas like numerical analysis for root-finding and solving linear systems, optimization techniques like gradient descent, numerical simulations in finite element methods and fluid dynamics, signal processing, machine learning model training, and data analysis. These methods balance computational effort with accuracy and flexibility.
