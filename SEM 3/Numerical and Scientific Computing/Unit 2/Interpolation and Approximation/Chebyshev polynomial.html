Chebyshev polynomial

Chebyshev polynomials are a family of orthogonal polynomials that have many applications in various fields of mathematics, science, and engineering

 Chebyshev polynomials are typically denoted as \(T_n(x)\) or  \(U_n(x)\), where \(n\) is a non-negative integer, and \(x\) is a real number.

There are two commonly used sequences of Chebyshev polynomials: the Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind \(T_n(x)\) and the Chebyshev polynomials of the second kind \(U_n(x)\).

  1. Chebyshev Polynomials of the First Kind \(T_n(x)\):
    The Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind are defined by the recurrence relation:
            \(T_0(x) = 1\)
            \(T_1(x) = x\)
            \(T_n(x) = 2x*T_{n-1}(x) - T_{n-2}(x)\) for  \(n \ge 1\)
    These polynomials have many important properties, including:
    • They are orthogonal with respect to the weight function \(w(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\) on the interval \([-1, 1]\).

    • They are used in various numerical analysis and approximation techniques, such as Chebyshev interpolation and Chebyshev spectral methods.
  2. Chebyshev Polynomials of the Second Kind \(U_n(x)\):
    The Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind are defined by the recurrence relation:
            \(U_0(x) = 1\)
            \(U_1(x) = 2x\)
            \(U_n(x) = 2x*U_{n-1}(x) - U_{n-2}(x)\) for  \(n \ge 1\)
    Like the first kind, these polynomials also have important properties and applications, though they are not orthogonal.
