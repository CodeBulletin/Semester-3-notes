Gauss Jordan

Gauss-Jordan elimination is an extension of the Gaussian elimination method used to solve systems of linear equations and perform matrix inversion. While Gaussian elimination focuses on converting a matrix into row-echelon form (upper triangular matrix), Gauss-Jordan elimination takes it a step further and transforms the matrix into Identity Matrxi, which is a simpler and more systematic way to find solutions or perform matrix inversion.

\(\begin{bmatrix}   a_{11} & a_{12} & | & 1 & 0 \\   a_{21} & a_{22} & | & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow \begin{bmatrix}  1 & 0  & | & b_{11} & b_{12} \\   0 & 1 & | & b_{21} & b_{22} \end{bmatrix}\)

where matrix B is inverse of A

\(BA = I\)

\(AA^{-1} = I\)

\(\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{bmatrix}\)
