Fitting a straight lines

Fitting a straight line to a set of data points, also known as linear regression, is a common application of the method of least squares. The general form of a straight line equation is \(y=mx+b\), where:

  • \(y\) is the dependent variable (the variable you want to predict),
  • \(x\) is the independent variable (the variable used for prediction),
  • \(m\) is the slope of the line, and
  • \(b\) is the y-intercept (the value of \(y\) when \(x\) is zero).
