Dependent Variables (Response Variables):

  • In an experimental or observational study, these are the outcomes or results that are being measured. They are thought to depend on or be influenced by another variable (the independent variable).

Independent Variables (Predictor Variables):

  • These are the variables that are manipulated or varied in an experiment to see how they affect the dependent variable. In observational studies, they are the variables that are believed to influence or predict the outcome.

Control Variables:

  • Variables that are kept constant or unchanged during an experiment to ensure that the effect on the dependent variable is due to the independent variable alone.

Confounding Variables:

  • Variables that are not of interest in the study but could affect the results, leading to incorrect conclusions if not accounted for.
