Mean

The mean, often referred to as the average, is a measure of central tendency in statistics. It's calculated by adding up all the values in a dataset and then dividing by the number of values. The mean provides a quick and simple summary of a set of numerical data. For example, if you have a set of numbers like 3, 5, 7, the mean is calculated as (3 + 5 + 7) / 3 = 5. The mean is widely used in various fields for its simplicity and ability to give a basic overall picture of a dataset's characteristics, but it can be sensitive to extreme values or outliers.
