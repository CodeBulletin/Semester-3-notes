Trapezoidal Rule

The Trapezoidal rule is a numerical integration method used to approximate the definite integral of a function. It is based on approximating the area under a curve by dividing it into trapezoids. The rule assumes that the function is approximately linear between adjacent points, and calculates the area of each trapezoid by taking the average of the function values at the two endpoints and multiplying it by the width of the interval. The sum of these areas gives an approximation of the integral. The accuracy of the approximation improves as the number of trapezoids increases. The Trapezoidal rule is a simple and commonly used method for numerical integration, especially when the function is not easily integrable analytically.
