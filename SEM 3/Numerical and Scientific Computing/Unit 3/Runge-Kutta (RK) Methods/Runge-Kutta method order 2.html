Runge-Kutta method order 2

The Runge-Kutta method is known for its accuracy and versatility, and there are various orders of Runge-Kutta methods available. The second-order Runge-Kutta method, often referred to as RK2 or the midpoint method, is one of the simplest and most commonly used variants.

Here's how the second-order Runge-Kutta method works:

Start with an initial condition: a known value of the solution at a specific point, often denoted as "x₀" and "y₀" for the independent and dependent variables, respectively.

Choose a step size, denoted as "h," which determines the distance between consecutive points in the approximation.

Use the initial condition to calculate the derivative of the function at the starting point: "f(x₀, y₀)." This derivative represents the slope of the function at that point.

Approximate the value of the solution at the midpoint of the interval using Euler's method:

y_midpoint = y₀ + (h/2) * f(x₀, y₀)

Calculate the derivative of the function at the midpoint: "f(x₀ + h/2, y_midpoint)."

Use the derivative at the midpoint to update the approximation:

y₁ = y₀ + h * f(x₀ + h/2, y_midpoint)

This equation represents the second-order Runge-Kutta update formula.

Repeat steps 3 to 6, using the newly approximated value as the initial condition, to calculate the solution at subsequent points. Continue this process until the desired range or number of points is reached.

The second-order Runge-Kutta method provides a more accurate approximation compared to Euler's method by considering the slope at the midpoint of the interval. However, it is still a second-order method, meaning that the error in the approximation decreases quadratically with the step size. For even higher accuracy, higher-order Runge-Kutta methods like RK4 can be used.
