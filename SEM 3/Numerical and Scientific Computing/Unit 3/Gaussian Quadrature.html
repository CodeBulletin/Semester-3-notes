Gaussian Quadrature

Gaussian Quadrature is an efficient numerical integration method that approximates a function's definite integral. It differs from methods like the Trapezoidal Rule or Simpson's Rule by strategically choosing points and weights for evaluation to maximize accuracy, especially effective for smooth functions. Key concepts include approximating the integral as a weighted sum of function values at specific points, with optimal points (nodes) and weights derived from the roots of orthogonal polynomials, like Legendre polynomials. This method adapts well to different intervals and is highly accurate for polynomials up to a certain degree, depending on the number of nodes. Gaussian Quadrature is ideal for high-precision applications in science and engineering, offering high accuracy with fewer evaluations.
