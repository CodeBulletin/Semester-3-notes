(a) What is digital media forensics?

Digital media forensics is a branch of digital forensics that focuses on the identification, collection, examination, and analysis of digital media. This includes images, audio, and video files. The goal is to uncover evidence and authenticate or debunk the digital media's integrity and origin, often in the context of criminal or civil investigations.

(b) Differentiate among Worms, Viruses, and Trojan Horses. 

  1. Worms: Self-replicating malware that spreads independently across networks. They exploit vulnerabilities in software to spread to other computers without human interaction. Example: Conficker Worm.
  2. Viruses: Malware that attaches itself to a program or file, enabling it to spread from one computer to another, typically requiring human action (like opening a file). Example: ILOVEYOU Virus.
  3. Trojan Horses: Disguised as legitimate software, Trojans trick users into installing them. Unlike viruses and worms, they don't self-replicate but can be just as destructive. Example: Zeus Trojan.

(c) Is there a difference between "cybercrime" and "cyberfraud"? Explain. 

  • Cybercrime is a broader term encompassing any criminal activity that involves a computer or network. It includes a wide range of offenses like hacking, online harassment, and identity theft.
  • Cyberfraud is a subset of cybercrime, specifically focusing on fraud committed using digital technology. This includes activities like phishing, credit card frauds, and online scams. Cyberfraud is always about deception for financial or personal gain, whereas cybercrime can have various motives.

(e) Differentiate between passive and active attacks with examples.

  • Passive Attacks: Involves eavesdropping on or monitoring systems without affecting them. Example: Sniffing network traffic to capture unencrypted data.
  • Active Attacks: Involves direct interference with the operations of a system or network. Example: DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks that overwhelm a system with traffic.

(f) What is a Blind SQL injection attack? 

A Blind SQL Injection attack is a type of SQL Injection where the attacker asks the database true or false questions and determines the answer based on the application's response. This method is used when the attacker doesn't receive direct data from the database.

(g) What is a virus hoax? 

A virus hoax is a false warning about a computer virus. Typically, it is a message warning the users of a non-existent virus, often causing unnecessary panic and can lead to misinformed or harmful actions, like deleting important system files.

(h) What do you understand by the salient features of the Indian IT Act?

The Indian Information Technology (IT) Act primarily addresses legal challenges related to internet usage, e-commerce, and cybersecurity. Key features include recognition of electronic records and digital signatures, provisions for cybercrime and data protection, and regulations for intermediaries and e-governance.

(i) Describe the main principles of information security?

  1. Confidentiality: Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized.
  2. Integrity: Maintaining the accuracy and consistency of data.
  3. Availability: Ensuring that information is available when needed.
  4. Non-repudiation: Guaranteeing that a person or entity cannot deny the authenticity of their signature on a document or a message they sent.

(j) What is the work of WIPO?

The World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) is a global forum for intellectual property (IP) services, policy, information, and cooperation. It is a self-funding agency of the United Nations, dedicated to developing a balanced and accessible international IP system. WIPO's work includes helping to shape international IP laws, providing services for protecting IP across nations, resolving disputes, and delivering capacity-building programs to enable effective use of IP.
