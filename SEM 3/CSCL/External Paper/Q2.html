(a) What is the cyber threat? How it is different from cybercrime? How do we classify cybercrimes? Explain each one briefly.

Cyber Threat

  • Definition: A cyber threat is a potential risk or danger to information or systems posed by the use of digital technologies and networks. It includes a variety of malicious activities that can compromise the security, integrity, or availability of digital assets.
  • Nature: Cyber threats are not necessarily actions but potential or identified risks that might lead to harmful activities.

Difference from Cybercrime

  • Cyber Threat: Broad and includes any potential harmful activity in the cyber environment. It encompasses various risks and vulnerabilities.
  • Cybercrime: Specific illegal activities conducted through digital means. It refers to actual criminal offenses involving computers and networks.

Cybercrimes can be classified into various categories based on the nature of the offense

  1. E-Mail Spoofing and Other Online Fraud
  2. Phishing
  3. Spamming

  6. Computer Sabotage
2. Cybercrime against property:
  1. Credit Card Frauds

  3. Internet Time Theft
3. Cybercrime against organization: 
  1. Unauthorized Accessing of Computer
  2. Password Sniffing
  3. Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks
  4. Virus
  5. E-Mail Bombing
  6. Salami Attack
  7. Logic Bomb
  8. Trojan Horse
  9. Data Diddling
  10. Industrial Spying
  11. Computer Network Intrusions
  12. Software Piracy
4. Cybercrime against society: 
  1. Forgery
  2. Cyberterrorism
  3. Web Jacking

(b) Explain the working of email spoofing. Discuss the preventive measures to protect from email spoofing.

Working of Email Spoofing

  • Process: Email spoofing involves forging an email header to make it appear as though it originated from a different source than the actual one. The “From” address is manipulated to show a familiar or trusted email address to the recipient.
  • Objective: The main goal is often to trick the recipient into believing that the message is from a legitimate source, which can be used for phishing, spreading malware, or fraud.

Preventive Measures

  1. Use of Email Authentication Methods: Implementing protocols like SPF (Sender Policy Framework), DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail), and DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance) can help in verifying the authenticity of the sender.
  2. Awareness and Vigilance: Educating users to recognize signs of spoofed emails, such as mismatched URLs, poor grammar, and unsolicited requests for sensitive information.
  3. Regularly Update Security Software: Keeping anti-virus and anti-malware software updated can help in detecting and preventing threats from spoofed emails.
  4. Verify Suspicious Emails: If an email seems suspicious, it’s advisable to verify it by contacting the sender through other means.
  5. Use of Secure Email Gateways: Deploying secure email gateways can filter out malicious emails, including those that are spoofed.
  6. Avoid Clicking on Suspicious Links or Attachments: Users should avoid interacting with any suspicious elements within emails that seem out of character for the supposed sender.
