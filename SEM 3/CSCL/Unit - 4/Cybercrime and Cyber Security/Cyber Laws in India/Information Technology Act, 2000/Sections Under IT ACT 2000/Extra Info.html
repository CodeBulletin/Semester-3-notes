Extra Info

Section 43 (Penalty for damage to computer, computer system, etc.): This section provides for compensation to the affected person without prescribing any imprisonment.

Section 66 (Computer-related offenses): The punishment can include imprisonment for up to three years, a fine which may extend to five lakh rupees, or both.

Section 66A (Punishment for sending offensive messages): This section, which was struck down by the Supreme Court in 2015, provided for imprisonment up to three years and a fine.

Section 66B (Receiving stolen computer or communication device): Imprisonment up to three years, or a fine which may extend to one lakh rupees, or both.

Section 66C (Identity Theft): Imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to a fine which may extend to one lakh rupees.

Section 66D (Cheating by personation): Imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to a fine which may extend to one lakh rupees.

Section 66E (Violation of privacy): Imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or a fine not exceeding two lakh rupees, or both.

Section 66F (Cyber terrorism): Imprisonment which may extend to imprisonment for life.

Section 67 (Publishing obscene material): First conviction with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years and with a fine which may extend to five lakh rupees and in the event of a second or subsequent conviction with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years and also with fine which may extend to ten lakh rupees.

Section 67A (Publishing sexually explicit material): Imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years and with fine which may extend to ten lakh rupees and in the event of a second or subsequent conviction with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years and also with fine which may extend to ten lakh rupees.

Section 67B (Publishing child pornographic material): Imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years and with fine which may extend to ten lakh rupees and in the event of a second or subsequent conviction with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years and also with fine which may extend to ten lakh rupees.

Section 72 (Breach of confidentiality and privacy): Imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years, or a fine which may extend to one lakh rupees, or both.

Section 72A (Disclosure of information in breach of lawful contract): Imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or a fine which may extend to five lakh rupees, or both.
