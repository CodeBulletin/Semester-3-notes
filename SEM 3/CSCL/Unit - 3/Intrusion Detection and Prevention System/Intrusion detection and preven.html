Intrusion detection and prevention techniques

Intrusion detection and prevention techniques are security measures used to identify and mitigate unauthorized access attempts or malicious activities within a computer network or system. Here are some commonly used techniques:

  1. Network-based Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS): NIDS monitors network traffic in real-time, analyzing packets and looking for suspicious patterns or known attack signatures. It can alert administrators when potential intrusions are detected.
  2. Host-based Intrusion Detection Systems (HIDS): HIDS operates on individual hosts or servers, monitoring system logs, file integrity, and other host-specific activities. It can detect unauthorized changes, suspicious processes, or abnormal behavior on a specific host.
  3. Firewalls: Firewalls act as a barrier between internal and external networks, controlling incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined security rules. They can block unauthorized access attempts and prevent certain types of attacks.
  4. Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS): IPS combines the functionalities of NIDS and firewalls. It not only detects potential intrusions but also actively blocks or prevents them from occurring by taking automated actions, such as dropping malicious packets or reconfiguring network settings.
  5. Security Information and Event Management (SIEM): SIEM systems collect and analyze log data from various sources, including network devices, servers, and applications. They correlate events and generate alerts based on predefined rules, helping to identify potential security incidents.
  6. Vulnerability Scanning: Vulnerability scanning tools scan networks or systems for known vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, or weak points that could be exploited by attackers. Regular scanning helps identify and patch vulnerabilities before they can be exploited.
  7. User Behavior Analytics (UBA): UBA tools analyze user behavior patterns to detect anomalies that may indicate unauthorized access or malicious activities. They can identify unusual login times, access patterns, or abnormal data transfers.
