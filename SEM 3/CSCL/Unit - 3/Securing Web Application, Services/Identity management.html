Identity management

Identity management (IAM) manages digital identities in an organization, covering user identities, authentication, authorization, and access to resources.

Key aspects include:

  1. User Provisioning: Creating and managing user accounts and access based on roles.
  2. Authentication: Verifying user identity, using passwords, biometrics, or other secure methods.
  3. Authorization: Determining user permissions based on authenticated identity and roles.
  4. Single Sign-On (SSO): Allowing one-time authentication for access to multiple systems.
  5. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Managing access rights based on predefined roles.
  6. Identity Federation: Enabling access across different organizations with existing credentials.
  7. Password Management: Implementing strong password policies and multi-factor authentication.
  8. Audit and Compliance: Tracking user activities for regulatory compliance and security.
  9. Identity Lifecycle Management: Managing the user's identity lifecycle from onboarding to offboarding.
  10. Identity Governance: Defining and enforcing policies related to identity management, focusing on business needs and compliance.
