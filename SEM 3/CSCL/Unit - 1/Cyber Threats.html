Cyber Threats

Cyber Squatting: Cyber squatting refers to the practice of registering or using domain names that are similar to existing trademarks or popular brands with the intention of profiting from the confusion or selling the domain back to the legitimate owner at an inflated price. It can lead to brand dilution, reputation damage, and financial losses for the legitimate trademark holders.

Cyber Warfare: Cyber warfare involves the use of cyber attacks by nation-states or state-sponsored groups to disrupt or damage the computer systems, networks, and infrastructure of other countries. It can target critical infrastructure, military systems, government networks, or even financial institutions. The aim is to gain a strategic advantage or undermine the operations and capabilities of the targeted nation.

Cyber Terrorism: Cyber terrorism refers to the use of cyber attacks by individuals or groups with political, ideological, or religious motivations to create fear, panic, or disruption. It can involve hacking into systems, defacing websites, spreading propaganda, or launching attacks that cause physical harm or economic damage. The goal is to achieve political or ideological objectives through the use of technology.

Cybercrime: Cybercrime encompasses a wide range of criminal activities conducted through the internet or computer networks. It includes activities such as hacking, identity theft, fraud, phishing, ransomware attacks, and online scams. Cybercriminals exploit vulnerabilities in systems and networks to gain unauthorized access, steal sensitive information, or extort money from individuals or organizations.

Cyber Offenses: Cyber offenses refer to illegal activities committed using computers or the internet that may not necessarily fall under the category of cybercrime. This can include activities such as unauthorized access to computer systems, distribution of malicious software, copyright infringement, and online harassment. While not all cyber offenses are considered criminal acts, they can still cause harm and may be subject to legal consequences.
