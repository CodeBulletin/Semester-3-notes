Cyber Stalking

Cyberstalking refers to the persistent and unwanted pursuit or harassment of an individual through online channels. It involves the use of digital communication platforms, such as social media, email, instant messaging, or online forums, to stalk, intimidate, threaten, or monitor the victim.

Cyberstalkers may engage in various behaviors to harass their victims, including:

  1. Unwanted Communication: Sending excessive, threatening, or harassing messages, emails, or comments to the victim. This can include explicit or offensive content, hate speech, or personal attacks.
  2. Monitoring and Surveillance: Cyberstalkers may closely monitor the victim's online activities, such as tracking their social media posts, checking their location through geolocation data, or monitoring their online presence through fake accounts or aliases.
  3. Impersonation: Cyberstalkers may create fake profiles or impersonate the victim online to spread false information, damage their reputation, or manipulate others' perception of the victim.
  4. Doxing: Doxing involves publicly revealing or publishing the victim's personal information, such as their home address, phone number, or workplace, with the intention of causing harm or facilitating further harassment.
  5. Online Threats: Cyberstalkers may make threats of physical harm, violence, or even death towards the victim. These threats can be conveyed through messages, emails, or public posts.
