Boosting

Boosting is an ensemble learning method enhancing machine learning accuracy by combining multiple weak learners (simple models) sequentially. Key aspects include:

  1. Sequential Training: Models are trained one after another, with each focusing on the errors of its predecessor. This differs from bagging's parallel training.
  2. Weak Learners: Often simple models like one-level decision trees. Individually weak, they perform effectively when combined.
  3. Weight Adjustment: After training, weights of data points are adjusted. Misclassified points gain weight, directing the next model to focus on these harder cases.
  4. Final Model: Combines weak learners' outputs through a weighted vote (classification) or sum (regression).
  5. Popular Algorithms: AdaBoost adjusts weights of misclassified points; Gradient Boosting minimizes a loss function; XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost are optimized gradient boosting versions.
  6. Advantages: Generally achieves higher accuracy, even with simple models.
  7. Challenges: Risk of overfitting, especially with noisy data; more computationally intensive than parallel methods like bagging.

In summary, boosting incrementally builds complex models from simpler ones, focusing on correcting earlier mistakes, and is effective in various machine learning tasks.
