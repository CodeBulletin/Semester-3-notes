Elbow Method

The Elbow Method is used in k-means clustering to determine the optimal number of clusters (k). The process includes:

  1. Computing Clustering for Various k Values: Perform k-means clustering for a range of k values, calculating the sum of squared distances from each point to its assigned center for each k.
  2. Plotting Results: Plot these sums of squared distances against k. The plot often resembles an arm with an elbow.
  3. Identifying the Elbow Point: The optimal k is at the 'elbow,' where increasing k does not significantly decrease the total variance. This point is where the plot bends.

The Elbow Method is heuristic and sometimes ambiguous, particularly if the plot lacks a clear bend. Alternative methods like the Silhouette Method or the Gap Statistic are also used for determining the optimal number of clusters.
