Market Basket Analysis

Market Basket Analysis (MBA) is a technique for analyzing customer purchase behavior by examining transaction records to find patterns in items bought together. Key points include:

  1. Association Rules: Identifying item pairings frequently bought together (e.g., bread and butter).
  2. Apriori Algorithm: A common algorithm in MBA that identifies frequent itemsets in a database.
  3. Metrics: Uses 'support' (frequency of itemset appearance), 'confidence' (likelihood of buying an item when another is bought), and 'lift' (normalized likelihood of two items being bought together).
  4. Applications: Useful in retail for product placement, cross-selling, and promotions. For example, placing bread near butter to boost sales.
  5. Challenges: Managing large datasets and interpreting rules with business acumen is crucial.

In essence, MBA helps retailers and marketers in product bundling, store layout, inventory, and targeted marketing strategies.
