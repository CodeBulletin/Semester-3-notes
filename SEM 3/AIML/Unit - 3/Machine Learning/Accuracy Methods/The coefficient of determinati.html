The coefficient of determination (R - Squared)

The coefficient of determination, R-squared (RÂ²), is a statistical measure for evaluating a regression model's fit. It indicates the variance proportion in the dependent variable explained by the model's independent variables, ranging from 0 to 1. A value of 0 means no explained variability, and 1 signifies perfect explanation.

R-squared assesses how well the model fits observed data, showing the proportion of response variable's variability due to the independent variables. However, a high R-squared alone doesn't guarantee model accuracy or causal relationships between variables. It's essential to also consider the significance of coefficients, model assumptions, and problem context.
