Overfitting and underfitting

Overfitting and underfitting are issues in machine learning related to a model's generalization to new data.

Overfitting: Happens when a model learns training data too closely, including noise, leading to great performance on training data but poor generalization to new data. It's marked by high training accuracy but low test accuracy, indicating the model is too tailored to training data.

Underfitting: Occurs when a model is too simple to capture data patterns, performing poorly on both training and new data. This is indicated by low accuracy on both training and test data, suggesting the model is overly generalized.

To combat overfitting, use regularization (adding a penalty to the objective function), cross-validation (splitting data to assess performance), or early stopping (halting training when validation performance drops).

For underfitting, increase model complexity, add features, or try more advanced algorithms, aiming for a balance between complexity and simplicity for optimal generalization.
