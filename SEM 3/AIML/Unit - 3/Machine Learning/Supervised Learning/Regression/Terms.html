Terms

Continuous Variables: Can take any value within a range, including decimals, and measured continuously. Examples include age, height, and income.

Target Variables: In supervised learning, these are variables predicted based on input features. In regression, they're continuous values; in classification, categorical labels.

Dependent Variables: Influenced by other variables, they're predicted or explained in regression analysis, also known as response or outcome variables.

Outcome Variables: Represent the results or outcomes in a study or analysis, particularly in predictive modeling or regression.

Feature Variables: Input variables in machine learning, providing information about data points. Can be continuous or categorical, also known as independent or predictor variables.

Independent Variables: Used to explain or predict dependent variables, manipulated or observed in experiments to see their effect on the outcome.
