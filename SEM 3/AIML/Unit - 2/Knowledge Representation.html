Knowledge Representation

Knowledge representation in artificial intelligence and cognitive science encodes information for reasoning and problem-solving, using Propositional Logic and First-Order Predicate Logic:

Propositional Logic:

  • Atomic Propositions: Basic true or false statements representing facts.
  • Logical Operators: Includes AND (∧), OR (∨), NOT (¬), IMPLIES (→), IF AND ONLY IF (↔) to form compound propositions.
  • Truth Tables: Show truth values of compound propositions based on atomic propositions.

First-Order Predicate Logic (FOL):

  • Constants and Variables: Constants signify specific objects, while variables represent any object within a domain.
  • Predicates: Describe relationships or properties, taking constants or variables as arguments.
  • Quantifiers: Universal (∀) and existential (∃) quantifiers express statements about all or some objects.
  • Functions: Represent operations or relationships producing new objects.
  • Connectives: Logical connectives like ∧, ∨, ¬, →, ↔ are used for complex statements.

Each approach has its own advantages, with the choice depending on the specific needs and complexities of the problem.
