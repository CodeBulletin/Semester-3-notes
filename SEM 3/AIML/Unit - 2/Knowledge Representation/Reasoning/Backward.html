Backward

Backward reasoning, or backward chaining, is a strategy in artificial intelligence and expert systems that starts with a specific goal and works backward to identify necessary supporting facts, rules, or conditions.

Key features:

  • Goal-Oriented: Begins with a defined goal or query.
  • Rule Application: Applies rules to the goal to find required supporting facts.
  • Work Backward: Traces back through rules and sub-goals to achieve the main goal.
  • Stop When Satisfied: Continues until known facts are reached or no further inference is possible.
  • Efficiency: Often more efficient when the goal is known and the focus is on finding the path to that goal.

Backward reasoning is used in expert systems, diagnostic systems, and other applications with well-defined goals to determine necessary conditions for achieving those goals.
