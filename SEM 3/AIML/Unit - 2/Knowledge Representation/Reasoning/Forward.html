Forward

Forward reasoning, also known as forward chaining, is an inference strategy in artificial intelligence and expert systems. It begins with known facts and applies rules to derive new information, following a data-driven, bottom-up approach.

Key features:

  • Data-Driven: Starts with initial data or facts.
  • Rule Application: Applies rules to existing facts to derive new information.
  • Incremental Inference: Adds new facts or conclusions incrementally.
  • Goal-Oriented: May be driven by specific objectives.
  • Data Expansion: Knowledge base grows as more facts are added.

Used in expert systems and logic programming, forward reasoning explores how rules lead to new conclusions, often in problem-solving or recommendation scenarios.
