Reasoning with uncertinity

Probabilistic reasoning is a method of dealing with uncertainty in information, making inferences based on probabilities rather than certainties. It's useful where information is incomplete or ambiguous.

Key Features:

  • Dealing with Uncertainty: Acknowledges that situations often involve varying degrees of certainty.
  • Use of Probabilities: Uses numerical likelihoods for various outcomes, often based on statistical data, past experiences, or models.
  • Bayesian Reasoning: A key method, it updates probability estimates for hypotheses with new evidence, based on Bayes' theorem.
  • Applications: Used in AI, machine learning, statistics, risk assessment, medical diagnosis, and financial modeling.
  • Handling Incomplete Information: Allows decision-making with the best available information.

Example: 

Imagine a medical diagnosis scenario where a patient shows certain symptoms. A doctor, using probabilistic reasoning, would consider the likelihood of various diseases that could cause these symptoms. If 70% of people with these symptoms have Disease A, 20% have Disease B, and 10% have Disease C, the doctor might initially lean towards Disease A. However, if a test rules out Disease A, the probabilities are updated, increasing the likelihood of Diseases B or C.

A knowledge representation where the concept of probability is applied to indicate uncertainty.

Need of probabilistic reasoning in AI:

  1. When there are unpredictable outcomes.
  2. When specifications or possibilities of predicates becomes too large to handle.
  3. When an unknown error occurs during an experiment. 

Probability: Probability can be defined as a chance that an uncertain event will occur. It is the numerical measure of the likelihood that an event will occur. The value of probability always remains between 0 and 1 that represent ideal uncertainties.
