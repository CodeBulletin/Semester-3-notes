Production Rule

Production rules, key in computer science, artificial intelligence, and expert systems, are conditional statements used in rule-based systems for decision-making and inference. They consist of:

  • Condition (Antecedent): Specifies criteria that trigger the rule, often as logical statements.
  • Action (Consequent): Details actions taken when the antecedent conditions are met.

Applications include:

  • Expert Systems: Capture expert knowledge and decision logic.
  • Business Rules: Automate decision-making in business systems.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Control behavior and guide decision-making.
  • Databases: Trigger actions or maintain data integrity.

Example: If a customer is over 18 and an item is in stock, then allow the purchase.

Production rules are vital for structured knowledge representation and adaptable decision-making systems across various domains.
