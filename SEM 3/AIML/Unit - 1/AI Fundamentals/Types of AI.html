Types of AI

Based on the capability and functionality, there are two types of AI:

  • Type-I 
  • Type-II

Type 1

Narrow or Weak AI (ANI):

  • Narrow AI, also known as Weak AI, is designed to perform specific tasks or solve particular problems.
  • These AI systems are highly specialized and excel in their predefined domains.
  • Examples include virtual personal assistants (e.g., Siri, Alexa), recommendation systems (e.g., Netflix recommendations), and autonomous vehicles.

General or Strong AI (AGI):

  • General AI, also known as Strong AI, represents a hypothetical form of AI that possesses human-level intelligence.
  • AGI systems would have the ability to understand, learn, and perform tasks across a wide range of domains, similar to how humans do.
  • Achieving AGI remains a significant challenge, and it does not currently exist.

Artificial Superintelligence (ASI):

  • Artificial Superintelligence is an advanced, theoretical form of AI that surpasses human intelligence in all aspects.
  • ASI would possess capabilities that are vastly superior to human intelligence and might be capable of solving complex problems, making critical decisions, and innovating at an unprecedented level.
  • ASI is purely speculative and has not been realized.

Type 2

Reactive Machines:

  • Reactive AI systems are designed to perform specific tasks based on predefined rules and programming.
  • They don't possess the ability to learn or adapt to new situations or information.
  • Examples include chess-playing programs like Deep Blue, which can make strategic moves based on predetermined rules.

Limited Memory AI:

  • Limited Memory AI systems have some capacity to learn and make decisions based on past experiences or data.
  • They can adapt to changing conditions to some extent.
  • Self-driving cars often use limited memory AI to navigate and respond to traffic conditions.

Theory of Mind AI:

  • Theory of Mind AI refers to a hypothetical AI system that could understand and interpret human emotions, intentions, and beliefs.
  • Such AI would be capable of more advanced social interactions and empathetic responses.
  • Development in this area is ongoing but not yet fully realized.

Self-aware AI:

  • Self-aware AI is a concept of AI systems that possess self-awareness and consciousness, similar to human beings.
  • This concept is highly speculative and remains a topic of philosophical and ethical debate.
