random restart hill climbing

Random Restart Hill Climbing is a modified hill climbing algorithm that addresses its limitation of getting stuck in local maxima by introducing random restarts.

Key Points:

  • Basic Hill Climbing: An iterative algorithm starting with a random solution, making incremental improvements.
  • Local Maxima Problem: Tendency to get stuck at local maxima, points higher than immediate neighbors but not the highest overall.
  • Random Restarts Solution: Runs the hill climbing algorithm multiple times from different random start points to avoid local maxima.
  • Process: Starts randomly, follows hill climbing steps, and restarts from a new random point when a peak is reached. Repeats until a satisfactory solution is found or a set number of times.
  • Advantages: Better chance of finding global maxima, simple to implement.
  • Disadvantages: Computationally expensive, no guarantee of finding the global maximum.

Random Restart Hill Climbing is effective in landscapes with many local maxima, providing a balance of exploration and exploitation.
