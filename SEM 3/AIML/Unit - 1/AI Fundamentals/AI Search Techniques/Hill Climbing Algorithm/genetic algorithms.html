genetic algorithms

Genetic algorithms (GAs) are evolutionary algorithms used for solving complex optimization and search problems, inspired by biological natural selection.

Key Elements:

  • Population and Individuals: Starts with a population representing potential solutions, encoded as strings of data.
  • Fitness Function: Evaluates how good each solution is, influencing selection for reproduction.
  • Selection: Chooses individuals for the next generation based on fitness, with a bias towards higher fitness but allowing some diversity.
  • Crossover (Recombination): Mixes genetic material of selected pairs to create offspring, similar to biological gene crossover.
  • Mutation: Introduces random changes to offspring to maintain variation and prevent suboptimal convergence.
  • New Generation and Iteration: Repeats selection, crossover, and mutation across generations, evolving towards optimal solutions.
  • Termination: Ends based on criteria like generation count, fitness level, or diversity.

GAs are effective for large, complex solution spaces and are used in engineering, economics, and more, excelling at escaping local optima.
