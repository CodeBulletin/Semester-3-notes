simulated annealing

Simulated annealing is an optimization technique that helps find an approximate global optimum in large search spaces, inspired by metallurgy's annealing process. It modifies the hill climbing algorithm to avoid getting stuck in local maxima.

Key Aspects:

  • Hill Climbing Algorithm: An optimization technique starting with an arbitrary solution and iteratively improving it.
  • Challenge in Hill Climbing: It struggles with local maxima, where it can get stuck.
  • Simulated Annealing Approach: Introduces a probability of accepting worse solutions, high initially and decreasing over time, to escape local maxima.
  • Cooling Schedule: A crucial element that dictates how fast the acceptance probability decreases.
  • Applications: Useful in various fields like operations research, computer science, and AI.

By incorporating simulated annealing, the hill climbing algorithm becomes more effective at exploring complex solution spaces and finding near-optimal solutions.
