Symbolic AI

Symbolic AI, also known as symbolic artificial intelligence or rule-based AI, is an approach to artificial intelligence that focuses on the use of symbols, rules, and logic to represent and manipulate knowledge and solve problems. In symbolic AI systems, knowledge is typically encoded using symbols and formal logic, allowing the system to reason, infer, and make decisions based on these symbolic representations.

Key characteristics of Symbolic AI include:

  1. Symbolic Representation: In symbolic AI, information and knowledge are represented using symbols, which can be words, numbers, or other abstract representations. These symbols are manipulated to perform tasks.
  2. Rule-Based Reasoning: Symbolic AI systems use explicit rules and logic to perform reasoning and decision-making. These rules define how the symbols are manipulated to draw conclusions or make inferences.
  3. Knowledge Base: Symbolic AI systems often have a knowledge base that contains a repository of facts, rules, and relationships. This knowledge base is used to answer questions and solve problems.
  4. Inference Engine: An inference engine is a component of symbolic AI systems that processes the rules and symbols to make logical deductions and reach conclusions.
  5. Declarative Knowledge: Knowledge in symbolic AI is typically declarative, meaning it describes facts and relationships without necessarily specifying how to use that knowledge. The system figures out how to apply the knowledge through its rules and reasoning processes.
  6. Transparency: Symbolic AI systems are often transparent and explainable, meaning it is possible to trace how a conclusion was reached by examining the rules and symbols used in the process.
