MAE

The Means-End Analysis (MEA) is a problem-solving approach in cognitive psychology and AI, involving these steps:

  1. Define Current State: Identify the present situation and obstacles to achieving the goal.
  2. Define Desired State: Specify the goal or outcome you aim to achieve.
  3. Identify Differences: Analyze what needs to change to reach the goal.
  4. Generate Subgoals: Break the problem into smaller, achievable steps that reduce differences between current and desired states.
  5. Plan and Execute: Develop and implement a plan to achieve each subgoal.
  6. Monitor Progress: Continuously assess and adjust the plan based on progress and challenges.
  7. Repeat: Continue this process until the desired state is achieved.

MEA helps in decomposing complex problems into manageable parts, guiding towards solutions through incremental steps. It's applied in fields like AI, psychology, and engineering for complex problem-solving and decision-making.
