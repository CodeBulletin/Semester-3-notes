(b) Define state spaces in AI. Why are they useful? Write state space for any suitable problem of your choice. 

In artificial intelligence, a state space represents the set of all possible states that a problem-solving agent can encounter while trying to reach a goal or solve a particular problem. It is a fundamental concept in various AI techniques, especially in search algorithms and problem-solving domains. The state space defines the environment or problem in terms of its possible configurations, and agents navigate through this space to find a solution or make decisions.

State spaces are useful in AI for several reasons:

  1. Problem Representation: State spaces provide a structured and organized way to represent complex problems, making them easier to analyze and solve.
  2. Search and Planning: State spaces are crucial for search algorithms like breadth-first search, depth-first search, A* search, and more. Agents can systematically explore the state space to find a solution.
  3. Problem Solving: By defining the state space, AI systems can break down a problem into manageable states and transitions, allowing for step-by-step problem solving.
  4. Decision-Making: State spaces help in modeling decision-making processes in AI, enabling agents to make informed choices by evaluating the consequences of different actions.
  5. Heuristic Evaluation: In many AI applications, state spaces are used in conjunction with heuristics to estimate the distance or cost to the goal state. This guides the search process efficiently.
